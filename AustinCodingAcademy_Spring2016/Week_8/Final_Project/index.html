After Hours

A Short Film

In the dead of night, a young black woman enters a convenience store with a loaded gun,
triggering a planned encounter with an unsuspecting white male.

We don’t know who she is, where she’s come from, or why she’s there. Once inside the store, the woman roams the aisles, clutching a purse that holds her gun. Outside, the man lingers in his van, tortured by the prospect of a missed connection with a woman that caught his eye. Suddenly, the woman grips her gun and starts walking quickly to the front of the store. Just as she does, the man re-enters. As the story unfolds, we realize things aren’t as they appear: the young black woman isn’t there for a robbery attempt, nor did the white man return for an innocent attempt at romance.

Race is at the forefront of contemporary American discourse.
It’s volatile and inflammatory, stirring tension and, most regrettably,
inciting violence.

We’re especially sensitive to appearances and perception in matters of race – perceived threats, perceived prejudices.

After Hours is intended in part to show how these appearances are just that: appearances.
They can deceive and mislead.

Filming this summer in Austin, TX.
